the hard part

The hard part isn't making. It's showing up.

So a Circle runs on a standing hour — and Drex spends most of its energy getting everyone to it. The kit:

the Orbone glance at your circles: amber = Circle Time · yellow = people inside
the Pulsethe editor’s chart: who’s missing · how alive · best week yet
& in the drawer:
a welcome note the moment you join
your bell comes pre-rung
your editor gets a nudge to greet you
a “what’s stirring” page — invites, never nags
a quiet who’s-missing list, so someone waves
a letter & the keys when you’ve earned editor

& when you want to run the hour better: letters.drex.style — field notes from Chielo, our in-house situated-learning expert

the whole app keeps your Circle's hour — it shows what your people have been making, then lights up all over when the room comes alive

a little about us

We exist to help people bring their gifts into the world.

At our core, we care most about bringing people in communion with one another.

A Circle works the way people have always gotten good at something: not from a teacher at the front of the room, but by making real things alongside others, at every level at once.

There's a name for it — situated learning — and it's what every Circle is built on. The zine you make together isn't homework; it's a record of who you're becoming.

It's also why we sweat the least glamorous part: showing up. You can't learn alongside people who aren't in the room — so Drex holds the door until they are.
