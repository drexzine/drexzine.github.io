btw

That loop has a name. It's called situated learning — Lave & Wenger, 1991 — and our founder spent his years at Stanford researching it. Midwives, tailors, butchers, quartermasters: Lave & Wenger studied how people actually get good at things, and it was never a classroom. We didn't invent it. We just built it.

the situated learning kit

How the magic happens.

Chielo's research says a craft needs three things to take root: people, virtual and material resources. So that's what's in the box.

There's a good deal more in there — a payphone, house calls, a rack of resources. You'll meet them once you're inside; naming them all here would just be a list.

Everyone chips in what they want to the circle fund — and the editor picks which power-ups it unlocks. You won’t be the one paying.

learning with each other

I researched situated learning at a Stanford makerspace, called the Product Realization Lab.

The finding is simple: people get good at things by making alongside other people — almost never by being taught at. Almost nothing built for the internet works that way. So we built Drex that way instead.

Your Circle is your community of practice. Your Zine is a record of who you’re becoming.
