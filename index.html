btw

That loop has a name. It's called situated learning — Lave & Wenger, 1991 — and our founder spent his years at Stanford researching it. Midwives, tailors, butchers, quartermasters: they studied how people actually get good at things, and it was never a classroom. We didn't invent it. We just built it.

the long game

The same people. Issue after issue.

Not a drop-in class. Not strangers cycling through. The same few faces for a year — who notice when you don't show, and who watch you get good.

You don't have to be here to learn.
Most circles are here to make.
But ship an issue a month, in front of people who'll actually see it, and the work gets better anyway. That's the whole trick.

show & tell with the world

Every challenge ends in a zine.

Your photos, your words, everyone's pages bound into one scrappy issue — an interactive zine a stranger can flip through, not a PDF. Cut and paste it by hand, or dump the pile and let the bot bind it.

A zine is one kind of exemplar — the thing a circle leaves behind. It's the first format we built, not the last. And every issue you're in stacks up into your portfolio.

but why not just…

Why not a group chat?

You already have one. Be honest about how it's going.

A chat is where a craft goes to not happen.
Discord is where you talk about your craft.
Drex is where you have one.

  • A course sells you a video you watch alone — if you ever finish it.
  • A community tool rents you an empty room and leaves the hard part to you.
  • Drex is the hour itself. Somebody sets the challenge, everybody makes, and it ends in something you can hold.
already run something?

Bring your group. Keep it alive.

You don't need us to find you people. You need the ones you have to show up.

Come in as the editor on day one. Send us your people, we send them keys — no queue, no approvals. Set the first challenge. By month's end there's an issue with everyone's name in it.

You stop being the one who nags.
The deadline does that now.

And you won't be the one paying. The circle keeps a small pool between them — a Discord boost, not a course fee. Skool, Circle and Meetup bill the organizer. We don't. A circle that chips in together turns up together.

More than fifteen? Say so — we'd sooner reshape the rooms than drop five friends.

who's behind it

Led by a researcher and two designers.

One of us studies how people actually get good at things. Two of us make things by hand for a living. That's the whole company — which is why it doesn't feel like software.

learning with each other

I studied situated learning at Stanford. The finding is unglamorous: people get good at things by making alongside other people — almost never by being taught at. Apprentices. Quilting bees. Darkroom crews.

Almost nothing built for the internet works that way. So we built Drex that way instead.

The zine isn't homework. It's a record of who you're becoming.
